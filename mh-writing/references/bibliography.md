# Bibliography

## Purpose

The referee is likely to read your reference list early in the review process, and may use it to assess your familiarity with and mastery of the topic. You never get a second chance to make a first impression: if the reference list is sloppily presented or misses key papers, the paper is headed towards the reject pile.

## Requirements

- Cite the papers the referee expects to see, including the foundational work in the area and the referee's own.
- Present every entry consistently across the list: the same fields, capitalisation, and formatting throughout.
- Every entry must name its venue, the conference or journal in which the work appeared. A reference with no venue is incomplete.
- A preprint must still name its venue: list it as an arXiv preprint together with its identifier (for example, arXiv:2401.01234). Never leave an entry with only a title and authors.
- Once a paper has appeared at a peer-reviewed venue, prefer that published version over the preprint, and update the citation accordingly.
