# Actionable Findings

## Purpose

It is increasingly important to state the actionable conclusions of your research: what will change in future research or practice (or both) as a result of your findings.

## Guidance

- Stating the actionable findings helps convey the possible impact of the research, and helps the referee understand why they should care about what you have discovered.
- If you find this section hard to write, it may be a sign that the research needs more work. Even if you decide not to include it, attempting to write it is a good intellectual discipline, and it may speed the transfer of your ideas into practice.
