# Problem Formulation

## Purpose

If you did a good job in the introduction and the problem is well understood, you may not need a separate problem formulation section. If the problem formulation is complicated, you may want to put it in a section of its own.

## Guidance

- Clearly separate concerns, so that the problem formulation is not tainted by any discussion of proposed solutions or evaluation.
- Make this the definitive formulation of the problem, collected in one place rather than scattered through the paper (defrag).
