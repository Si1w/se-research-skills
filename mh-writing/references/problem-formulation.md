# Problem Formulation

## When Needed
- If the introduction already does a good job and the problem is well-understood, a separate section may not be necessary.
- If the problem formulation is complicated, put it in its own section.

## Separation of Concerns
- The problem formulation must not be tainted by discussion of proposed solutions or evaluation.
- The formulation here should be the definitive one (defrag — do not scatter bits of the formulation across other sections).
