# Experimental Setup

## Purpose

Most software engineering papers have some kind of evaluation. This section explains how you propose to go about demonstrating that your solution addresses the problem. The section may have other names.

## Content

- The subjects on which you chose to evaluate.
- The statistical tests or other measurements and techniques that you applied.
- The research questions you set out to ask, together with the motivation for asking them.

## Separation of Concerns

- Do not pollute this section with results or with details of the proposed solution; keep it entirely about how you evaluated your approach.
- As a sanity check, ask whether you could write the same section about someone else's proposed solution to the same problem, without knowing anything about their results. If you can, you have probably got the separation of concerns about right.
