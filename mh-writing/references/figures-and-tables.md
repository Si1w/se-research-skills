# Figures and Tables

## Figures
- Must be readable even when reduced to fit. Increase point size of labels (axis labels, legends) relative to figure size.
- **Semantic Information Principle (SIP)**: every discernible visual difference should convey meaning. If two boxes have different shapes, that difference must mean something.
- Avoid superficial features that do not convey meaning: shading behind boxes, background colours on plots.

## Tables
- **Numeric columns**: always right-justified.
- **Name columns** (e.g. program names): left-justified.
- **Centre justification**: rarely appropriate — only when data does not require left or right alignment.
- **Units**: in column headings, not repeated in each row.
- **No unnecessary repetition**: if you see the same word repeated in every row, it belongs in the heading. Like programming: repeated code means you need a loop.

## Overall Look
- **BW principle**: the paper must be perfectly understandable when printed in black and white. Referees usually see things in B&W.
- **Paragraph breaks over pages**: as a last step, arrange paragraphs to complete neatly at column ends, and place headings at the top of columns where possible.
