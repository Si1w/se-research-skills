# Conclusion and Future Work

## Conclusion
- Restate the primary contributions and findings.
- The key difference from the abstract/introduction: the conclusion can freely use technical terms that have already been defined in the paper, without forward references.
- Keep it as short as possible without underselling the contribution.

## Future Work
- Should be your genuine view about what the next steps are and why they are important.
- Can be what you intend to do, or what you hope others will do.
- **Keep it short.** A long future work discussion signals that the paper has underachieved — it has left undone what ought to have been done, and the length itself tells the referee so.
