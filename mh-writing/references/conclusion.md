# Conclusion and Future Work

## Purpose

The conclusion and future work section is typically hard to write. The conclusion can restate the primary contributions and findings of the paper.

## Conclusion

- Unlike the abstract and introduction — which can only include technical terms that they define or forward-reference, both of which are cumbersome — the conclusion already has these terms available, so it can state the contributions directly.
- Keep the conclusion as short as possible without underselling the contribution, and avoid forward references.

## Future Work

- State your genuine view about what the next steps are and why they are important — whether what you intend to do yourself, or what you hope others will do as a result of reading your work.
- Keep it short. A long future work discussion is a sure indication that the paper has underachieved, leaving undone that which ought to have been done.
