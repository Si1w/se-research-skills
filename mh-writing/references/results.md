# Results

## Purpose

The results section contains the results from your evaluation, presented with the aid of tables and figures.

## Structure

- Present the results first, and then provide explicit answers to each research question separately. This separates the evidence, presented without judgement, from the authors' view on how that evidence answers the questions, which generally involves judgement.
- Aim for a one-to-one correspondence between figures/tables and the research questions they address. If this becomes many-to-one, consider refactoring or reconsidering your research questions; subsidiary research questions (RQ1.1, RQ1.2, etc.) may help.

## The Idealised Structure

A particularly clear paper is structured so that the overall claims in the introduction map directly onto top-level research questions (with subsidiary questions where appropriate); each research question is answered conclusively at a single point in the paper, with reference to a single table or figure; and from those tables or figures the Zeller numbers are selected and included in the abstract. Aim for this ideal and then justify any deviation, but do not follow it so slavishly that it gets in the way of the overall message or the clarity of the evidence.
