# Title

## Purpose

The title is the first 'abstract' of your paper that the referee reads, and it is part of the paper's 'envelope' that forms the first impression. A good title is memorable, which increases citations, discussion, and conversational mentions of the work.

## Guidance

- Think carefully about the title, and be ready to change it if you can find a better one.
- Do not make it catchy at the price of irritating or confusing the referee.
- Give the technical contribution a pithy, evocative name. If the naming is right, others will copy it; if it is wrong, excellent results may go unnoticed. Refine the name until it conveys the exact meaning you intend, and avoid prepositions and other incidental words.
