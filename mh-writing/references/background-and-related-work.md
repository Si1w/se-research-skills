# Background and Related Work

## Two Options (Rarely Both)
1. **Related Work section** — located just before the conclusions.
2. **Background/Preliminaries section** — located just after the introduction. Use when the paper requires significant scene-setting (definitions, mathematical notations, establishing state-of-the-art).

## Preferred: Related Work Section
- Set the scene for state-of-the-art in the introduction.
- Delay detailed discussion of related work to the end of the paper.
- Explain not only what other authors have done, but how their work relates to your contributions.
- Conclude the related work section with a single pithy paragraph summarising the novelty and advance your paper contributes.
- This is easier after the reader has already seen the body of your paper.

## When to Use Background Section Instead
- When you need survey-like material to establish the importance of your problem or the gap in the literature.
- When the section introduces new notations, definitions, or contributes a new survey — this provides novelty, so it is acceptable.

## Avoid "Novelty Delay"
- A background section that merely describes what others have done delays the referee's access to your novel material.
- This can have a bad psychological effect on the referee.
- If your background is just a description of prior work, move it to a related work section.
